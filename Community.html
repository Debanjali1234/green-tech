Community Stories

Inspiring stories from the world of biotech and green tech

Urban Greenhouses Transforming Cities

In several cities worldwide, communities have embraced urban farming by transforming rooftops and vacant lots into greenhouses. These urban greenhouses provide fresh produce, reduce the carbon footprint associated with food transportation, and create green jobs. One notable example is the Brooklyn Grange in New York City.

Community Solar Gardens

Solar gardens are community-based solar power installations that allow multiple participants to benefit from the energy produced by a single solar array. A standout example is the Colorado Community Solar Gardens Act, which has led to the creation of numerous solar gardens.

Biotech Startups Tackling Plastic Waste

Innovative biotech startups are developing solutions to address plastic pollution. One such company is BioCellection, founded by young scientists Miranda Wang and Jeanny Yao. BioCellection uses biotechnology to transform plastic waste into valuable industrial chemicals.

Community Composting Initiatives

Composting organic waste is an effective way to reduce landfill usage and create nutrient-rich soil for gardening. In San Francisco, the community-led initiative Recology has successfully implemented a curbside composting program.

Bioremediation Projects in Local Waterways

Bioremediation uses microorganisms to clean up contaminated environments. In the Gowanus Canal in Brooklyn, the Gowanus Canal Conservancy is working on bioremediation projects to address pollution in this historic waterway.

Vertical Farming in Urban Areas

Vertical farming is an innovative approach to agriculture that involves growing crops in vertically stacked layers, often using hydroponics or aeroponics. Companies like Plenty and AeroFarms are leading the way in urban vertical farming.

Green Building Initiatives

Communities are increasingly adopting green building practices to create sustainable, energy-efficient housing. The Bullitt Center in Seattle is an example of a community-driven green building project.

Marine Conservation and Biotech

In coastal communities, biotech solutions are being used to restore marine ecosystems. Coral Vita, a company based in the Bahamas, grows resilient corals in land-based farms and transplants them into degraded reefs.
